My Story

How I Got Here

A curious engineer who accidentally became an entrepreneur and educator.

Hi! I'm Rajath, and I wear quite a few hats. Currently, I'm the CEO of Pace Lab, but that's just one part of my journey. With a BTech in engineering and an MBA, I started out like many others—until I noticed something that bothered me.

I kept meeting brilliant engineering students who felt completely unprepared for the real world. That gap between classroom theory and actual skills kept me up at night. So, I decided to do something about it. Over the past five years, I've had the privilege of working with over 30,000 students, helping them bridge that gap.

Along the way, I've been invited to speak at places like IIT Bombay, IIT Kanpur, and ISRO—which still feels surreal! I'm also involved in tech and real estate ventures, and Times of India once featured my startup concepts, which was pretty cool.

What I'm Building

Pace Lab: My Attempt to Fix Education

Starting with lab kits, now helping students learn skills that actually matter.

Pace Lab started when I was frustrated with how disconnected traditional engineering education was from real-world skills. We began by creating hands-on lab kits for colleges. Today, we've grown into something I'm really proud of—a platform that helps students learn technologies they'll actually use in their careers.

AI & Data Science

I'm fascinated by AI and love helping students understand not just the theory, but how to actually build things with machine learning and data analytics.

Cybersecurity & Cloud

Security is so important today. We focus on practical skills—ethical hacking, cloud security, and understanding how to protect systems in the real world.

Robotics & IoT

There's something magical about building robots and IoT devices. We help students create solutions that solve actual problems, not just classroom exercises.

App Development

Everyone has an app idea! We teach students how to turn those ideas into reality, including advanced image processing and mobile technologies.

VR & Emerging Tech

The future is immersive. We explore VR, AR, and other emerging technologies that will shape how we work and play in the coming years.

Entrepreneurship

Having been through the startup journey myself, I love mentoring aspiring entrepreneurs and helping them avoid the mistakes I made.

What Keeps Me Going

I'm not trying to change the world overnight, but I do believe we can make education more relevant and help young people find their path. Every student who lands their first job or starts their own company reminds me why this work matters.

My goal is simple: help people discover what they're capable of and give them the tools to make it happen.
